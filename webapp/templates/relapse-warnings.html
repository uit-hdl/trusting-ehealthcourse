{% trans "Warnings of Relapse" %}

{% trans "Warnings of relapse refer to early signs or indicators that an individual who is recovering from mental illness or an unhealthy behavior may be at risk of returning to that behavior. Recognizing these warnings can help the individual or their support network take proactive steps to prevent a full relapse." %}

{% trans "Common warnings of relapse include:" %}

  1. {% trans "Changes in Behavior: Sudden shifts in routine, neglect of responsibilities, or reverting to old habits." %}
  2. {% trans "Emotional Instability: Increased irritability, anxiety, depression, or mood swings." %}
  3. {% trans "Social Withdrawal: Avoiding support groups, family, or friends who are supportive of recovery." %}
  4. {% trans "Negative Thinking: Engaging in self-defeating thoughts or rationalizing that one can control the behavior." %}
  5. {% trans "Loss of Interest: Losing interest in activities that were once enjoyable or helpful in recovery." %}
  6. {% trans "Physical Signs: Changes in sleep patterns, appetite, or overall health." %}
  7. {% trans "Cravings or Urges: Increasing thoughts or desires to engage in the addictive behavior." %}
