{% trans 'Negative Symptoms of Schizophrenia' %}

{% trans 'Negative symptoms of schizophrenia refer to the absence or reduction of normal behaviors and functions that are usually present in healthy individuals. These symptoms reflect a loss of abilities and can significantly impair a person\'s daily functioning and quality of life. Unlike positive symptoms, which add abnormal experiences or behaviors, negative symptoms take away normal capabilities and emotional responses.' %}

  1. {% trans 'Reduced expression of emotions: Also known as flat affect, this is where a person\'s face remains immobile, and they speak in a dull or monotonous voice.' %}
  2. {% trans 'Reduced feelings of pleasure in everyday life: This refers to anhedonia, where the individual experiences a decreased ability to feel pleasure from activities that were once enjoyable.' %}
  3. {% trans 'Difficulty beginning and sustaining activities: Known as avolition, this is characterized by a lack of motivation to start or maintain tasks, including everyday activities.' %}
  4. {% trans 'Reduced speaking: Also called alogia, this involves a significant reduction in the amount and content of speech, often resulting in brief and empty replies.' %}
